## Features Requiring Attention

The main attention areas are in `Client.csv`.

- High missingness: `ownrent`, `lor`, `dwlltype`, `numbcars`, `HHstatin`, `dwllsize`
- Medium missingness: `prizm_social_one`, `adults`, `infobase`, `income`
- Low but non-zero missingness across several household and device fields, including `avg6mou`, `avg6qty`, `avg6rev`, `hnd_price`, `truck`, `rv`, and the kid-related fields
- In `Record.csv`, missingness is limited and stays below 1% for every affected feature, so it is lower risk than the gaps in `Client.csv`

## Possible Business Meaning of Missing Values

- Missing demographic or household fields in `Client.csv` may mean the customer did not provide the information, the field was not collected for that segment, or the data source could not be matched reliably.
- High missingness in `ownrent`, `dwlltype`, `HHstatin`, and `dwllsize` suggests these attributes may be optional, externally appended, or only available for a subset of customers.
- Low missingness in device and usage fields likely reflects sparse capture rather than a structural data issue.
- The small missingness in `Record.csv` may come from billing-cycle gaps, incomplete usage aggregation, or records with limited activity in the observation window.

## Initial Data Quality Assessment

- `Record.csv` is relatively clean from a missing-data perspective.
- `Client.csv` has substantial missingness in several customer profile features, so those columns need careful handling before modeling.
- The missingness is uneven rather than uniform, which suggests the gaps are systematic and may carry business meaning.
- No imputation has been applied yet, so downstream work should decide whether to drop, encode, or impute by feature group.
